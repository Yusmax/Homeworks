TT pistol

Description: Fedor Tokarev was a renowned Russian firearms designer best known for creating the TT-33 pistol. The TT-33, developed in the early 1930s, served as a standard-issue sidearm for the Soviet military during and after World War II. This semi-automatic pistol was chambered in 7.62x25mm Tokarev and gained a reputation for its robust construction and reliability in harsh conditions. The TT-33 featured a simple blowback design, a single-action trigger, and an eight-round detachable magazine. Its design influenced many subsequent pistols, and it saw widespread use in Eastern Europe and other regions. Tokarev's pistol design is often regarded as a classic in the world of firearms, and its influence can still be seen in modern handgun designs.
